
Program Structure:

Theory: 100 Hours

Practical: 200 Hours (includes 40 hours of training at the Technology Hub; the remaining 160 hours delivered through the Outreach Center)





Detailed Module Plan

Module 1: Fundamentals of AI & Engineering Concepts
Theory Topics:
AI history and evolution, symbolic vs. neural AI
Robotics basics: sensors, actuators, and control systems
Engineering principles for problem-solving using AI

Practical Tasks:
Build simple robots with STEM kits
Program basic motion and obstacle avoidance

Module 2: AI and Augmented/Virtual Reality
Theory Topics:
Fundamentals of AR/VR and mixed reality
Role of computer vision in AR/VR
Enhancing immersion using AI-generated environments

Practical Tasks:
Build AR/VR scenes using Blockly or Unity-based tools
Train simple AI to interact within virtual environments



Module 3: Python Programming & AI Logic Development
Theory Topics:
Python basics: variables, loops, data types
AI logic: conditionals, states, and search algorithms
Supervised learning vs. unsupervised learning

Practical Tasks:
Program logic-based games with block coding & Python
Implement mini-projects using scikit-learn or TensorFlow Lite

Module 4: Humanoid Robotics & AI Motion Planning
Theory Topics:
Vision-based navigation & face detection
NLP for interaction with humanoid robots
Motion planning algorithms (A*, Dijkstra)

Practical Tasks:
Program NAO/Alpha Mini for speech interaction and movements
Facial expression mimicry using AI models

Module 5: AI for Smart & Autonomous Transportation
Theory Topics:
Self-driving car architecture
AI for route optimization & collision prediction
Drone-based data analysis

Practical Tasks:
Program Zumi car to navigate real-world layouts
Simulate traffic signals and autonomous drone delivery



Module 6: AI for Industry 4.0 & Manufacturing
Theory Topics:
Cyber-physical systems and predictive maintenance
AI for quality control & defect detection
Digital twins and simulation with AI

Practical Tasks:
Use Dobot for pick-and-place operations
Train AI models for object classification on the line

Module 7: AI in IoT
Theory Topics:
AI + IoT architecture and data pipelines
Edge AI, TinyML, and sensor integration
Security and encryption in smart devices

Practical Tasks:
Build smart home devices with AI triggers
Deploy ML models on microcontrollers (ESP32/Raspberry Pi)

Module 8: AI & Projected Reality (Spatial Computing)
Theory Topics:
Projection mapping and AI interactions
Gesture and speech control in projected interfaces
AI in education and digital twins

Practical Tasks:
Create interactive learning environments
Control AI avatars using real-time projections



Module 9: AI in Esports and Game Development
Theory Topics:
NPC behavior modeling and reinforcement learning
Game AI frameworks and emotion-based design
Real-time analytics for Esports performance

Practical Tasks:
Build multiplayer AI agents
Analyze game performance using tracking data

Module 10: AI Ethics, Explainability, and Prompt Engineering
Theory Topics:
Ethical AI, bias detection, and fairness
Explainable AI (XAI) models and tools
Introduction to Prompt Engineering for GenAI tools

Practical Tasks:
Use LIME/SHAP for model explanation
Build prompts for ChatGPT/Bard to automate academic/research tasks

Capstone Project
Objective: Cross-functional project integrating at least two stations (e.g., IoT + Robotics or AR/VR + Game Dev)
Deliverables: Final report, deployment-ready prototype, demo video, and pitch deck
